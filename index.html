Frequently Asked Questions

The adminstration,logistics and management of the event are the priorities of event planning.
On the other hand the concept or theme of a event is referred to as event design.

Planning an event may take anything from weeks to months, depending on the size and scope of the event. Because attendees have generally confirmed their participation and no information needs to be disseminated to the public, private and corporate events take less time. When planning a public event with a capacity of 100 to 1,000 people, the details should be made public and available to the media two months ahead of time. People should be exposed to the content at least a couple times before the event, both online and offline. This also provides ample time for the media to incorporate the material into their reports.

Cancellations made seven days or more before the event date will get a full refund. There will be a 20% cancellation charge if you cancel within 3-6 days. Cancellations made less than 48 hours before the event will be charged a 30% cancellation fee.

Examine each and every expenditure. From the start, make a list of everything you intend to spend or need to spend on your event. Setting goals and objectives. Discuss your strategy on a frequent basis.
